6 Resume & Interview Tips

How to structure an AI resume

  • Headline + one-liner: role target + strongest proof (years, domain, stack).
  • Impact bullets: metrics (latency, accuracy, cost, revenue, time saved) > generic duties.
  • Projects section: problem → approach → stack → result → link/demo.
  • Skills buckets: Languages, ML/GenAI, Data, MLOps/Cloud — keep it truthful and scannable.

Top 10 interview questions (typical AI roles)

  1. Walk me through an end-to-end ML or GenAI system you’d design for <X>.
  2. How do you prevent data leakage and overfitting in practice?
  3. Explain precision, recall, and when you’d optimize for each.
  4. How does RAG work — and when would you not use it?
  5. How do you evaluate LLM applications (offline vs online)?
  6. Describe how you’d deploy and monitor a model in production.
  7. Tradeoffs: fine-tuning vs RAG vs prompt engineering.
  8. How do you handle API failures, rate limits, and retries?
  9. Tell me about a project that failed — what did you learn?
  10. How do you collaborate with PMs/design on AI UX and risk?

How to talk about AI projects

  • Context in 20 seconds: user, constraint, success metric.
  • Decision log: why this architecture, what alternatives you rejected.
  • Honest limits: bias, eval gaps, cost — shows maturity.

LinkedIn profile optimization

  • Banner + headline: target role, niche (FinTech, SaaS, GCC), and proof.
  • Featured: flagship project, blog/note, or talk.
  • About: outcomes and stack; avoid buzzword soup.
  • Activity: comment thoughtfully on 2–3 posts/week in your niche.
